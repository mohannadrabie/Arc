The Technical Excellence Pillar emphasizes the importance of structuring of software solutions for team collaboration. That is to say, the main goal is to foster cooperation between engineers in the planning, building, and maintaining of solutions by focusing on the consistency and adaptability of their designs.

As a general rule, the software design principles applied during solution design at the class or entity level should be applied to high level components defining an architecture within a system to produce one that is similarly robust, scalable, and understandable. This is because the problems that emerge in managing the relationships between and within the inner parts of an architecture are often scaled up versions of the same problems that are solved by classical software engineering design patterns and principles.
